Cumulative Net Investment Since 1950

Running total of (investment - depreciation).
Key finding: The 2025 investment-to-depreciation ratio of 0.73x matches the worst levels of the 2016–2017 downturn. Farmers are replacing only 73 cents of equipment for every dollar that wears out. Expanding back to the 1950s reveals that the E/C ratio was below 0.7 for most of that decade during rapid farm consolidation.

The serious farming equipment is barely turning over. At 2024 sales rates, 46 years to replace 100+ HP tractors and 54 years to replace combines. Even at the 2021 boom peak, large tractors were on a 36-year cycle — 2.4x their recommended 15-year life.

Census: Market Value of Equipment on Farms

Self-reported market value by farm operators.
Price inflation is masking the real decline. Real investment peaked at $48.7B in 2014 and has fallen to $28.2B in 2025 — a 42% decline in actual equipment volumes. Equipment prices have risen 29% since 2017 (and 108% since 1997), far outpacing CPI (+31%) and GDP deflator (+29%). Meanwhile, the corn price index is at 131 vs. its 2022 peak of 206 — revenue is falling while costs keep rising.

Data Sources & Methodology

  • Investment: BEA/FRED C268RC1A027NBEA (nominal) & C268RX1A020NBEA (real). FRED
  • Depreciation: BEA/FRED BOGZ1FA136330023Q (quarterly SAAR, annualized). FRED
  • Net Stock: BEA/FRED BOGZ1FL135013265Q. FRED
  • Prices: BEA/FRED B268RG3A086NBEA (ag machinery), CPIAUCSL (CPI), A191RD3A086NBEA (GDP deflator), PMAIZMTUSDM (corn). FRED
  • Census: USDA Census of Agriculture Tables 44 & 45. Quick Stats
  • Trade: UN Comtrade (2000-09), Census Bureau (2010-24). USITC
  • Sales: AEM retail reports. AEM
  • Equipment Life: Iowa State Extension A3-29. ISU

Methodology: E/C ratio = gross nominal investment / annualized capital consumption. "Implied replacement cycle" = Census stock / AEM annual retail sales. Most reliable for 100+ HP where virtually all sales go to farms. All price indices rebased to 2017=100.

Data compiled February 2026. Built from publicly available U.S. government data (BEA, USDA, Census Bureau) and industry sources (AEM).
