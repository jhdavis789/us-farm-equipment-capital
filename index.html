The Investment Gap

Gross investment in agricultural machinery vs. capital consumption (depreciation). When investment falls below depreciation, the fleet is shrinking in real terms.

Gross Investment vs. Depreciation

BEA geometric declining-balance depreciation: δ = R/T where R ≈ 1.65, T = 15-year service life ⇒ ~11% annual rate applied to current-cost net stock.

Investment-to-Depreciation Ratio

Ratio > 1.0 = fleet renewal. Ratio < 1.0 = fleet shrinking.

Cumulative Net Investment Since 1950

Running total of (investment − depreciation). Does not account for used equipment exports.

Rolling & Decay-Weighted Net Investment

L10Y = sum of last 10 years net. Decay-weighted uses exponential decay (half-life 7 years) so recent investment counts more.
Key finding: The 2025 investment-to-depreciation ratio of 0.73x matches the worst levels of the 2016–2017 downturn. Farmers are replacing only 73 cents of equipment for every dollar that wears out. Expanding back to the 1950s reveals that the E/C ratio was below 0.7 for most of that decade during rapid farm consolidation.

Replacement Cycle Over Time (by HP Class)

Using 2022 Census fleet counts and AEM annual sales for each year (2017–2025).
The serious farming equipment is barely turning over. At 2024 sales rates, 46 years to replace 100+ HP tractors and 54 years to replace combines. Even at the 2021 boom peak, large tractors were on a 36-year cycle — 2.4x their recommended 15-year life. The 2025 collapse to 204 years for combines and 70 years for 100+ HP tractors is the worst on record.

Census: Equipment Value — Total, Per Acre, Per Bushel-Equivalent

Price inflation is masking the real decline. Real investment peaked at $48.7B in 2014 and has fallen to $28.2B in 2025 — a 42% decline in actual equipment volumes. Equipment costs per harvested acre have risen from $451 (2002) to $1,037 (2022), while per-bushel equipment intensity has climbed from $7.67 to $11.87 — farmers are spending 55% more capital per unit of output.

Data Sources & Methodology

  • Investment: BEA/FRED C268RC1A027NBEA (nominal) & C268RX1A020NBEA (real). FRED
  • Depreciation: BEA/FRED BOGZ1FA136330023Q (quarterly SAAR, annualized). Geometric declining-balance: δ = R/T ≈ 1.65/15 = 11%/yr. FRED
  • Net Stock: BEA/FRED BOGZ1FL135013265Q. FRED
  • Prices: BEA/FRED B268RG3A086NBEA (ag machinery), CPIAUCSL (CPI), A191RD3A086NBEA (GDP deflator), WPS012202 (corn PPI). FRED
  • Interest Rates: FRED FEDFUNDS (effective fed funds rate), GS10 (10-year Treasury). FRED
  • Census: USDA Census of Agriculture Tables 44 & 45. Quick Stats
  • Trade: UN Comtrade (2000-09), Census Bureau (2010-24). New/used split: Schedule B .1010 (new) / .1090 (used). USITC
  • Sales: AEM retail reports. AEM
  • Equipment Life: Iowa State Extension A3-29 (15-yr / 10,000-12,000 engine hours). ISU

Methodology: E/C ratio = gross nominal investment / annualized capital consumption. "Implied replacement cycle" = Census stock / AEM annual retail sales. BEA depreciation uses geometric declining-balance at rate δ = R/T (R ≈ 1.65 declining-balance factor, T = 15-yr service life). This is a statistical estimate — actual depreciation depends on hours of use, maintenance, and operating conditions. All price indices rebased to 2017=100. Corn-equivalent bushels: 1 bu soybeans = 2.5 bu corn-eq, 1 bu wheat = 1.2 bu corn-eq (long-run price ratios).

Data compiled February 2026. Built from publicly available U.S. government data (BEA, USDA, Census Bureau) and industry sources (AEM).
